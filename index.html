Protein 101

Protein and amino acids are some of the main components of our muscles, bones, skin, tissues, and organs. When we take in protein, our body breaks it down into individual amino acids during the digestion phases and then uses the amino acids extracted to create new proteins where needed. Protein induces the feeling of fullness, more so than both carbs and fat. This can be greatly beneficial for athletes or anyone with a hard working job who are often working and needing to fuel their bodies for long stretches of time.

Along with appetite reduction, consuming protein will temporarily boosts metabolism. The body uses this energy to digest and make use of the broken down nutrients in food. This is called "TEF" or the thermic effect of food, and proteinâ€™s thermic effect is much higher than that of carbohydrates and fat. These are just a few of many reasons that monitoring protein intake can be an absolute game changer.

For many years most have had to intake protein in food forms or via a protein shake that doesn't taste all too well. Now with the advancement in the market there are many ways to make protein foods, drinks, and even deserts taste like it's too good to be true!
